Scenario 2¶

Popuation probabilities = 0.2 and 0.5 Popuation probabilities = 0.3 and 0.5
Popuation probabilities = 0.4 and 0.5
Sample sizes = 100 , 100

Compute the t-Test.

Expecations:
As the population probabilities converge the t-statistic will decrease the the p-value will increase.

In [8]:
from scipy.stats import ttest_ind

n1 = 10
n2 = 10
p1 = 0.2
p2 = 0.5

pop1 = np.random.binomial(n1, p1, 10000)
pop2 = np.random.binomial(n2,p2, 10000) 

sample1 = np.random.choice(pop1, 100, replace = True)
sample2 = np.random.choice(pop2, 100, replace = True)

print(ttest_ind(sample2, sample1, equal_var=False))

Ttest_indResult(statistic=16.167046082790648, pvalue=1.3086394014213975e-37)

plt.axvline(np.mean(sample_means_1), label = np.round(np.mean(sample_means_1),2), color='red',\
                                                      linestyle='solid', linewidth=2)
plt.hist(sample_means_2, density = True, alpha=0.5)

plt.show()

When changing from a binomial distrbution to a Poisson distribution the sample means still represent the population means. As the population mean for set 1 is 35.0 and the sample mean converges to 35. Likewise the population mean for set 2 is 45.0 and the sample mean converges to 45.
